About

My work sits between computational design and physical prototypes, with a focus on computational textiles and soft systems. I use machine knitting and digital fabrication to build structures that can sense, heat, or scaffold biological growth, and I pair that with rigorous engineering workflows—instrumentation, logging, analysis, and iteration—so the behavior is measurable and repeatable.

Alongside lab work, I've built strong foundations in algorithms, systems, and computational fabrication. That toolkit helps me treat fabrication pipelines like software: versioned, reproducible, and designed for iteration. My portfolio is organized around a clear spine—computational textiles + soft systems—supported by methods in simulation, optimization, and machine learning.

If you're a researcher, the Research page summarizes my themes and links to the most relevant projects. If you're a recruiter, the fastest way to evaluate my work is through the project sections and the linked repos/demos.
